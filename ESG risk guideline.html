Supplier ESG Risk Assessment Guideline

Loob Holding is committed to responsible and sustainable sourcing practices. Our Supplier ESG Risk Assessment evaluates suppliers’ environmental, social, and governance (ESG) performance to ensure alignment with our sustainability standards and to identify areas for improvement. The assessment enables Loob to identify potential ESG-related risks in our supply chain, support suppliers in improving their ESG maturity, and strengthen long-term, responsible partnerships.

1. Introduction

Loob Holding is committed to responsible and sustainable sourcing practices. Our Supplier ESG Risk Assessment evaluates suppliers’ environmental, social, and governance (ESG) performance to ensure alignment with our sustainability standards and to identify areas for improvement. The assessment enables Loob to:

  • Identify potential ESG-related risks in our supply chain.
  • Support suppliers in improving their ESG maturity.
  • Strengthen long-term, responsible partnerships.

2. Overview of ESG Risk Assessment Process

Each supplier’s ESG risk is evaluated through five key components, combining operational exposure with ESG performance:

2.1 Score Components

  • Sales Score – Annual sales value to Loob Holding.
  • Product/Service Score – Criticality and dependency level of the supplied category.
  • Environmental Score – Self-declared environmental practices and initiatives.
  • Social Score – Self-declared social responsibility and workforce practices.
  • Governance Score – Self-declared governance, ethics, and compliance practices.

2.2 Scoring Scale

Score Risk Description
1 High risk / Weak ESG practice
2 Moderate risk / Partial compliance
3 Low risk / Strong ESG practice

3. Methodology of Scoring

The overall ESG risk score is derived using the following formula:

ESG Risk Score = (Product/Service score × w1) + (Sales score × w2) + (Environmental score × w3) + (Social score × w4) + (Governance score × w5)

where w₁–w₅ represent the internal weightage assigned to each component. Each “w” value is determined by Loob Holding based on materiality and internal risk prioritisation.

4. Scoring Matrix

4.1 Product/Service Category Risk Score

Product/Service Category Score Risk Justification
Food & Beverage Ingredients 1 High ESG exposure due to raw material sourcing, food safety, traceability, and environmental impact (e.g., agriculture, livestock).
Packaging 2 Medium ESG impact linked to material use, recyclability, and waste management.
Logistics & Supply Chain Services 2 Moderate ESG exposure such as transport emissions and driver working conditions.
Equipment & Machinery 3 Limited ESG footprint during use; risks mainly arise in upstream manufacturing.
Services 3 Minimal ESG exposure; typically office-based or professional services.

(Higher sales exposure indicates greater operational dependency and potential ESG risk.)

4.3 ESG Risk Score

Suppliers complete a self-declaration form comprising questions across three ESG pillars. Responses are scored (1–3) based on the maturity and implementation of practices.

ESG Pillar Assessment Focus
Environmental Waste management, energy use, emissions control, environmental policies.
Social Labour conditions, workplace safety, diversity and inclusion, community engagement.
Governance Business ethics, anti-corruption, data privacy, regulatory compliance.

5. ESG Risk Categorisation & Recommended Action

The final ESG Risk Score determines the supplier’s risk category and the corresponding follow-up action by Loob Holding:

ESG Risk Category Score Description Recommended Action
High Risk 1.0 – 1.7 Supplier has limited ESG practices or awareness; may present reputational or operational risk. Prioritised for engagement, corrective action plan, or re-evaluation based on ESG gaps identified.
Moderate Risk 1.8 – 2.4 Supplier shows partial ESG compliance; key areas for improvement exist. Encouraged to enhance ESG practices through capacity building, guidance, or monitoring.
Low Risk 2.5 – 3.0 Supplier demonstrates strong ESG performance and alignment with Loob’s sustainability standards. Recognised as preferred partner; may be featured as a benchmark supplier for ESG excellence.

6. Frequency and Data Handling

  • Assessments are conducted annually or during onboarding or contract renewal.
  • Supplier data is treated confidentially and used solely for internal ESG evaluation.
  • Suppliers may update their ESG data periodically or share certification updates.

7. Continuous Improvement

Loob Holding works collaboratively with suppliers to improve overall sustainability performance. Suppliers identified with higher risk levels are provided support and engagement opportunities to strengthen their ESG capabilities.
